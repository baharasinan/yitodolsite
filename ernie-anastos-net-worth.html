Ernie Anastos’ Finance Overview

Career Beginnings and Early Earnings

Ernie Anastos began his illustrious career in radio, initially working under the name Ernie Andrews in Boston. He made a significant leap to television in 1976, starting as an anchor in Providence.

His major break came in 1978 when he joined WABC-TV in New York. Here, he anchored the 11:00 pm Eyewitness News broadcast, marking the start of a successful television career. His early earnings laid a solid foundation for his future wealth.

Major Career Achievements and Their Financial Impact

Ernie’s career is highlighted by his coverage of monumental events. Notably, he reported on the World Trade Center attacks and Princess Diana’s funeral, events that not only showcased his journalistic prowess but also significantly enhanced his profile.

His work has earned him over 30 Emmy Awards, including the Best Newscast in New York and a nomination for the Edward R. Murrow Award. These accolades have played a crucial role in boosting his market value and net worth.

In 2005, Anastos signed a lucrative $10 million contract with WNYW-TV. This deal significantly increased his income and solidified his position as a top news anchor.

Additional Sources of Income

Beyond his salary as a news anchor, Ernie has diversified his income streams. He authored the book Twixt: Teens Yesterday and Today and wrote numerous columns for Family Circle.

Additionally, he owned several radio stations through the Anastos Media Group, which were later sold to the Empire Broadcasting Corporation.

Ernie also ventured into television production, hosting and producing shows like Positively America. His on-camera roles in Hollywood films further supplemented his earnings.

Personal Life and Its Influence on Financial Decisions
