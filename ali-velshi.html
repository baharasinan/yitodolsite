Who is NBC Chief Ali Velshi?

Ali is an author and television journalist based in Canada. He has been working as the Chief of NBC News. 

People recognize Velshi from his work at CNN. He was the senior business correspondent of CNN before and co-hosted CNN International World Week. Ali is busy working as the live anchor at MSNBS as well. 

Biography Timeline

Ali Velshi is a Canadian television journalist and anchor, born on October 29, 1968, in Nairobi, Kenya. Velshi has worked for various media outlets, including CNN, Al Jazeera America, and MSNBC. He is known for his extensive coverage of politics, finance, and economics. Here is a detailed biography timeline for Ali Velshi.
  • 1996: Ali Velshi started his journalism career as a general assignment reporter for CFTO in Toronto, Canada.
  • 1999: As a business correspondent, he joined Report on Business Television (ROBTv) in Canada.
  • 2001: Velshi joined CNN in the United States as a business reporter and anchor.
  • 2005: He became the co-host of CNN’s “Your Money” with Christine Romans.
  • 2011: Velshi joined Al Jazeera America as a host and correspondent.
  • 2016: He returned to MSNBC as a senior economic correspondent and anchor.
  • 2017: Velshi was named the co-anchor of MSNBC’s “Velshi & Ruhle” with Stephanie Ruhle.
  • 2020: He began hosting his show, “Velshi,” on MSNBC.
  • 2021: Velshi won a News and Documentary Emmy Award for his reporting on the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Age, Height, Birthday, Parents 

Ali was born to Murad and Mila on October 29, 1968. They welcomed him to the world in Nairobi, Kenya. The family shifted to Canada shortly, so Ali grew up in Ontario. Ali’s father became the first man of Indian descent to get a spot at the Legislative Assembly of Ontario. 

Velshi attended Northern High School, and for college, he went to Queens University in Ontario. The journalist studied religious students back in 1994. 

 

Career

Ali started working in Toronto as a CFTO initially. He secured the reporter role at Cable Plus 24 and City TV later. After two years working there, Ali shifted to the U.S.A., where he got into CNN Business News Channel. Later, the people at CN made him a business reporter and broadcaster.

During his time on CNN, at one point, Velshi hosted a reality TV show, The Turnaround, which was thirteen hours long. He joined the American Island after CNN. 

Only in 2016 did Ali join NBC News. He wrote and published the book “Gimme My Money Back: Your Guide To Overcoming the Financial Crisis.
