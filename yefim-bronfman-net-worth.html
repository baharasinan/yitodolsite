FAQs about Yefim Bronfman

Who is Yefim Bronfman?

He is an internationally recognized pianist known for his commanding technique and exceptional lyrical gifts. He regularly performs at major festivals and with leading orchestras worldwide.

When did Yefim Bronfman immigrate to Israel?

Bronfman moved to Israel in 1973 when he was 15 years old.

Where did Yefim Bronfman study music?

He studied at the Rubin Academy of Music at Tel Aviv University, Juilliard School, Marlboro School of Music, and the Curtis Institute of Music.

Has he won any notable awards?

Yes, he is a recipient of the Avery Fisher Prize and the Jean Gimbel Lane prize in piano performance from Northwestern University.

With which famous conductors has Bronfman worked?

He has collaborated with many esteemed conductors including Daniel Barenboim, Zubin Mehta, and Riccardo Muti, among others.

What are some of Bronfman’s notable recordings?

His recordings include the complete Prokofiev concerti with the Israel Philharmonic Orchestra and Zubin Mehta, and the three Bartok Piano Concerti which won a Grammy in 1997.

Has he appeared in any films?

Yes, he was featured in Disney’s Fantasia 2000, performing Shostakovich’s Piano Concerto No. 2.

What kind of music does he typically perform?

Bronfman performs a wide range of repertoire from Beethoven and Brahms to Prokofiev and Shostakovich.

Is he involved in chamber music?

Yes, he is an avid chamber musician and has performed with notable artists like Isaac Stern and Pinchas Zukerman.

What is Yefim Bronfman’s teaching involvement?
